The Boychild Crisis

Too many boys are becoming men with no one showing them how

Absent fathers, peer pressure, a culture that confuses manhood with money or bravado, and no safe space to ask honest questions. This is what boys across Uganda and Africa, and in the diaspora, are growing up inside of. Boyhood does not pause while a family decides when the time is right.

The Crisis

What boys are actually up against

1

Absent Role Models

Many boys are being raised without a present father or consistent male figure to show them what responsible manhood looks like.

2

No Safe Space

Few places exist where a boy can be honest about fear, confusion, or identity without being mocked or dismissed.

3

A Confused Definition of Manhood

Peer pressure and a culture obsessed with money and bravado often fill the gap that mentorship should fill instead.

This is the gap Boys to Men Mentorship Camp exists to close. Here is why families like yours are enrolling now.

For Parents

Speaking directly to you

You know exactly what your son is up against.

The pressures of peer influence, unemployment anxiety, and a culture that often confuses manhood with money or bravado are real, and close. Boys to Men gives your son a safe, structured space with mentors who understand this context because they live in it too.

What you get: a local, accessible programme, transparent costs, mentors you can meet and trust, and a follow-up relationship that continues long after camp ends.

See local intakes

Give your son the home he only half remembers.

Raising a son abroad often means raising him without the extended family, culture, and rites of passage that shaped his father or grandfather. Boys to Men gives your son a genuine, structured reconnection to Uganda and to men actively invested in who he becomes.

Built for your timing: the Boys to Men Summer Camp runs 9 days every July in Kampala, timed to school summer breaks, for UGX 990,000 or USD 250. The next Summer Camp runs July 7–16, 2027.

See the Summer Camp

You are already doing the hardest job alone. This part, you do not have to.

Raising a son without a present father figure does not mean he goes without positive male mentorship, it means finding it deliberately. Boys to Men exists to be exactly that: vetted, consistent, trustworthy male mentors.

What you get: full transparency on who mentors your son, a safe and structured environment, and an ongoing relationship. This applies whether you are based in Uganda, elsewhere in Africa, or in the diaspora.

For Boys

This part is for you, not just your parents

You might be reading this because a parent sent you the link, or because you found it yourself. Either way: Boys to Men is not a punishment, and it is not a school trip. It is a space where you get to figure out who you actually are, away from the noise, with men who have been where you are and will not talk down to you.

Whether you are in Kampala, Arua, Gulu, or growing up somewhere far from Uganda, there is a place for you here.
