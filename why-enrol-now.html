Why Enrol Now

The right mentorship, at the right time, changes everything

Boyhood does not pause while a family decides when the time is right. Every year a boy spends without direction, without a trusted mentor, or without a safe space to ask honest questions is a year that shapes who he becomes. Here is why families like yours are enrolling now.

Speaking directly to you

Choose the voice that fits your situation. Each one addresses the real questions you are likely asking.

You know exactly what your son is up against.

The pressures of peer influence, unemployment anxiety, and a culture that often confuses manhood with money or bravado are real, and close. Boys to Men gives your son a safe, structured space with mentors who understand this context because they live in it too, not an imported programme, but one built for the world he is actually growing up in.

What you get: a local, accessible programme, transparent costs, mentors you can meet and trust, and a follow-up relationship that continues long after camp ends.

Apply for the next local intake

Give your son the home he only half remembers.

Raising a son abroad often means raising him without the extended family, culture, and rites of passage that shaped his father or grandfather. Boys to Men gives your son something rare: a genuine, structured reconnection to Uganda and to a community of men actively invested in who he becomes, not a holiday, a transformation.

What you get: a dedicated diaspora enquiry path, clear logistics before travel, regular updates during camp, and a mentor relationship that continues after he returns home.

Start a diaspora enquiry

You are already doing the hardest job alone. This part, you do not have to.

Raising a son without a present father figure does not mean he goes without positive male mentorship, it means finding it deliberately. Boys to Men exists to be exactly that: vetted, consistent, trustworthy male mentors who model the character and responsibility you are already working hard to instil.

What you get: full transparency on who mentors your son and how he is supervised, a safe and structured environment, and an ongoing relationship, not a one-off event. This applies whether you are based in Uganda, elsewhere in Africa, or in the diaspora.
